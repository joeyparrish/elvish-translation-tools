# Sindarin Grammar Reference

Source: https://www.elvish.org/gwaith/sindarin_grammar.htm

## Verbs

- **Imperative**: ending `-o`, all persons. `daro!` "halt", `linno!` "sing".
- **Gerund / infinitive**: `-ed` after `-e-` stem basic verbs, `-ad` after
  `-a-` stem derived verbs. `peded` "to speak/speaking", `linnad` "to sing/
  singing".
- **Present** (continuative): stem + lengthened vowel + `-a`. `pîda` "he is
  speaking".
- **Present** (aorist): stem + `-i`. `pedin` "I speak".
- **Past**: suffix `-nt` (becomes `-nn-` before pronominal endings).
  `pennen` "spoke", `pennin` "I spoke".
- **Present participle**: basic verbs `-el` (or `-iel`), derived `-ol`.
  `pedel`, `linnol`.
- **Past participle**: `-en` (singular), `-in` (plural with i-affection).
  `pennen / pennin`, `linnen / linnin`.
- **Causative**: traditionally formed with `-tha-` suffix in Quenya;
  in Sindarin, prefixing or compounding with a verb of doing/making (e.g.
  `nor-` "run" → `northa-` "cause to run, make run") is one approach.
  Imperative `northo` "make run, play (video)".

## Nouns: pluralization (i-affection)

Vowel changes propagating from final `-i`:

| Vowel | Non-final | Final |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| a     | e         | ai    |
| o     | e         | y     |
| u     | y         | y     |
| au    | oe        | oe    |

Examples: `aran` → `erain` "kings"; `onod` → `enyd` "ents"; `orch` → `yrch`
"orcs"; `naug` → `noeg` "dwarves".

**Class plurals** (collective): `-ath` (e.g. `Argonath` "the royal stones"),
`-rim` "people", `-hoth` "host, folk" (often pejorative).

## Word order

- Articles precede the noun: `i vellon` "the friend" (singular `i` lenites);
  `in gaurhoth` "the werewolves" (plural `in` nasal-mutates).
- Adjectives follow the noun and undergo soft mutation:
  `annon edhellen` "Elvish gate". Indefinite/interrogative/exclamative
  adjectives precede.
- Genitive: word order (`Ennyn Durin` "Doors of Durin"); the genitive
  article `en` (singular) / `in` (plural) is used for common nouns
  (`Cabed-en-Aras` "Deer's Leap").

## Articles

- Singular: `i` "the" — triggers lenition.
- Plural: `in` "the" — triggers nasal mutation.
- Genitive: `en` (sg) / `in` (pl) — `en` triggers mixed mutation per Salo.

## Pronouns

**Independent / nominative**: `im` or `nin` "I"; `mên` / `men` "we";
`chân` "you (pl)"; `le` "thou"; `e` / `san` "he/it"; `ti` / `sain` "they".

**Verb suffixes**: `-n` "I", `-m` "we", `-ch` "you", `-l` "thou", `-r` "they".

**Possessive**: `nîn` "my", `chîn` "your", `tîn` "his/her", `mîn` "our".

**Demonstrative**: `sen` (pl. `sin`) "this"; `tan` (pl. `tain`) "that".

## Derivational affixes

- `-ad` / `-ed`: gerund / verbal noun.
- `-on`: augmentative ("great X"). `sirion` "great river".
- `-eg`: diminutive. `nogotheg` "dwarflet".
- `-ui`: adjectival "having the quality of". `eithui` "edged, sharp".
- `-weg`: adjectival, possessive-quality, debated as productive.
- `-rim` / `-hoth` / `-waith` / `-lir`: collective people/folk suffixes.
- `pen-`: privative prefix, "without X". `penadar` "fatherless".
- `al-` / `alfir-`: negative / privative. `alfirin` "immortal".
- `ú-`: standard negation. Triggers mutation.
- `ad-`: "again, re-". `adnortha-` "run again, replay".
- `at-` / `ata-`: "double, two-". With mutation: `at-` + `t-` → `ath-`.

## Practical translation notes

- Sindarin negation uses `ú-` (with mutation) or independent `law` / `baw`.
  Do NOT use Quenya `la` (a common mistake).
- "Language" in Sindarin is `lam` (no attested plural; Neo-Sindarin
  reconstruction follows regular i-mutation: `laim`), not Quenya `lambë`.
- "Open" is Sindarin `pant`, not Quenya `panta`.
- For "in/within", use `mi` (no mutation) or `vi` (lenited), not unattested
  forms like `bir`.
- Use `daro` (attested imperative "halt", as spoken by Haldir in LotR) for
  "stop" / "pause", not coined forms.
